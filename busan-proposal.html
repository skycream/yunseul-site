The Haeundae proposal, in full.

The building, the floor, the neighborhood and the numbers behind unit 416 of the Haeundae La Muette tower, 620 Haeundae-ro, Busan.

Enquire Property overview

The building.

Address
620 Haeundae-ro, Unit 416

The Haeundae La Muette tower, at exits 5 and 7 of Haeundae Station, at the entrance of the Gunam-ro pedestrian axis to the beach.

Building
42 floors + 5 basement

A 155-meter landmark completed in 2015, with about 60,570 m² of floor area on a 4,317 m² site.

Composition
Retail, cinema, hotel

Retail units on B1 to 4F, the six-screen CGV multiplex across 2F to 5F, 477 serviced residences on 7F to 39F and penthouses above.

The unit
69.4 m² net (747 sq ft)

Exclusive-use area, 21 pyeong, on the 4th floor. Measured plan and unit photos available with the property sheet.

Station link
Direct connection

Haeundae Station meets the building at the basement concourse, and escalators carry visitors up through the retail floors.

Parking
B1 to B5 garage

Self-park garage across five basement levels, shared with the hotel and cinema.

A floor that brings its own visitors.

Unit 416 sits on the 4th floor, the same floor as CGV screens 1 to 3: cinema-goers ride the tower's escalators up past the retail units every day of the week, from a multiplex of 1,032 seats.

Above, 477 serviced residences operated as the Felix by STX hotel keep short and long-stay guests in the building year round. Below, the station concourse feeds the podium directly.

The podium, as planned.

Between the station and the sand.

The building stands 500 meters from Haeundae Beach, straight down Gunam-ro, the district's main pedestrian street, with the station at its base.

The pedestrian axis

The tower stands at the entrance of Gunam-ro, facing the Haeridan-gil cafe district across the road. Foot traffic between the station and the beach passes the front door.

The year-round district

Haeundae's festivals, film events and hotel row keep the area busy in all four seasons, and the Centum City business cluster is one subway stop away.

The building's own economy

Hotel guests, cinema-goers and commuters overlap in one tower. A 4th-floor unit here reaches audiences a standalone building never sees.
