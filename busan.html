A space on Korea's best-known coast.

Haeundae draws visitors from across Korea and Asia all year. This unit sits in a tower connected directly to Haeundae Station, with a hotel operating in the same building and the beach a short walk away.

One district, four engines.

The beach economy

Haeundae Beach is the most famous stretch of coast in Korea, anchoring festivals, film events and a hotel row that keeps the district full in every season, not just summer.

Centum City next door

One subway stop away sits Busan's business and media cluster: the BEXCO convention center, tech and media offices, and the Shinsegae Centum City department store, one of the largest in the world.

An international community

The district concentrates Busan's expat residents, international schools and foreign-friendly services. Korea's digital nomad visa, launched in 2024, is pulling in remote workers on top.

Reaching it is easy

Busan Metro Line 2 runs through the district, Gimhae International Airport connects the region across Asia, and KTX trains link Busan Station to Seoul in under three hours.
